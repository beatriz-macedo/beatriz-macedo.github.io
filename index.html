About me

I am a postdoctoral researcher in Computational Immunology, passionate about the intersection of microbial genomics and vaccine development. My research focuses on probiogenomics, recombinant lactic acid bacteria, and the in silico analysis of beneficial microorganisms such as Weissella paramesenteroides.

I conducted my PhD at UFSC and participated in COVID-19 research initiatives in Minas Gerais. I have experience in cloning, ELISA, and bioinformatics, and my work bridges computational analysis with experimental validation.

Outside the lab, I appreciate literature, science communication, and exploring connections between microbiota and human health.
